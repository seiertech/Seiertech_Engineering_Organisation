---

## 1. Purpose

This authority governs the creation, maintenance, and enforcement of all engineering standards within the SeierTech EMS. Standards are derived from authorities and define detailed engineering expectations against which artefacts and work products are verified.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to STD-000001 through STD-000005 and all future standards created within the EMS.

---

## 3. Principles

- STD-001 — Standards are derived from authorities — never invented independently.
- STD-002 — Every standard is enforced by the Standards Engineer persona.
- STD-003 — No standard may conflict with AUTH-001.
- STD-004 — Standards are versioned. Breaking changes increment the major version.
- STD-005 — STD-000001 through STD-000005 are foundational and may not be deprecated without constitutional revision.

---

## 4. Requirements

| ID | Requirement |
|---|---|
| STD-REQ-001 | Every standard SHALL conform to STD-000003 Standard subclass structure |
| STD-REQ-002 | Every standard SHALL declare its governing authority |
| STD-REQ-003 | STD-000001 quality gates SHALL apply to all artefacts without exception |
| STD-REQ-004 | Vocabulary from STD-000004 SHALL be used exclusively across all EMS artefacts |
| STD-REQ-005 | Traceability per STD-000005 SHALL be declared in every artefact |
| STD-REQ-006 | New standards SHALL be approved by Executive Director before ACTIVE status |

---

## 5. Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Standards Engineer | Enforce all standards against all artefacts |
| Executive Director | Approve new standards and constitutional changes |
| Documentation Curator | Maintain standards currency and accessibility |

---

## 6. Governance

New standards require Executive Director approval. Standards conflicts escalate to Executive Director. STD-000001 through STD-000005 require constitutional revision to change.
