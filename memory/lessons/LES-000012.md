## Finding
`EMS_OPERATING_MODEL.md` Section 8's Relationships table stated `Cites | AUTH-001 through AUTH-010 | All current Authorities` and `Cites | REG-000001 through REG-000010 | All current Registers`. A programmatic check of which authority/register IDs actually appeared anywhere in the document's body found only 6 of 10 authorities (AUTH-004, 005, 006, 009 missing) and 6 of 10 registers (REG-000003, 004, 005, 007 missing) genuinely cited. The document — itself produced specifically to correct an external review's finding about doctrine coherence — contained an uncaught false self-claim.

## Impact
A document whose entire purpose is to be the authoritative synthesis of the EMS contained a claim about its own completeness that was not true. This is a more serious instance of the same class of risk the document exists to prevent — undetected because no prior check had compared a document's stated citations against its actual content.

## Reuse Guidance
Any synthesis document that claims to cite "all current X" should be programmatically checked against the actual current set of X, not just trusted at the point of writing — completeness claims drift the moment a new artefact is added after the claim was made, even without anyone editing the claiming document.
