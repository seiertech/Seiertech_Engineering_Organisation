## Finding
A persona sense-check earlier this session (`PERSONA_SENSE_CHECK_REPORT.md`) found and fixed a real structural gap — 8 of Team 1's 25 personas missing 11 of 19 standard sections. That fix was verified and confirmed clean across all 50 personas. Separately, when directly asked about content *quality*, sampling several personas' AI Reasoning Profiles (including ones already confirmed structurally complete) found the actual reasoning instructions were generic procedural bullets — "flag CVEs above MEDIUM severity," "identify the actual architecture as it exists" — that could plausibly apply to a templated version of the role with light editing, not encoded domain expertise. A real Security Architect needs to know what makes a CVE actually exploitable versus theoretical, what a genuinely dangerous JWT misconfiguration looks like, what counts as a CRITICAL versus a LOW severity finding and why — none of which the original profiles specified.

## Impact
Structural completeness and content depth are different claims. A persona can pass every structural check (all sections present, correctly formatted) while still producing shallow, generic reasoning when actually invoked — because nothing in the structural check examines whether the *content* of the AI Reasoning Profile encodes genuine expertise versus templated procedure. This had never been checked until directly asked.

## Reuse Guidance
When assessing "quality" or "depth" of any artefact class, explicitly distinguish structural completeness (do the required sections exist) from content depth (is what's actually written in those sections genuinely expert-level, specific, and falsifiable) — these require different check methods and conflating them produces false confidence. A structural check is mechanical and can be automated; a content-depth check requires actually reading the content against what a real domain expert would need to know, which is closer to the work performed in this amendment than to a script.
